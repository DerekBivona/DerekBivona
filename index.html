What's Up World!

I'm Derek. I'm a researcher, engineer, and washed-up baseball player who is enthusiastic about machine learning and biomechanics. Currently, I work as a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Cardiovascular Medicine Department at the University of Virginia (UVA) Health System. I use machine learning to predict the long-term survival of patients with heart failure who undergo pacemaker therapy. I received my PhD in Biomedical Engineering from UVA where I developed biophysical and statistical models for predicting the progression and regression of cardiac growth. I enjoy answering difficult questions that often require collobaration and continuous learning. When I'm not writing code or staring at medical images, I'm either drinking iced coffee, lifting weights and doing handstands in the gym, or winning championships in flag football, ultimate frisbee & softball with my friends.

Projects

  • Machine Learning for
    CRT Response

    During my PhD and postdoc, I developed a machine learning model to characterize multidimensional response to cardiac resynchronization therapy (CRT) and its relationship with long-term survival. Check out the model here (with username: 'tester' & password: 'BilchickCRT')!

  • Finite-Element Models
    of Myocardial Infarction

    As part of my PhD, I contructed finite-element models of the left ventricle (LV) that accounted for changes in composition, mechanics, and growth following a myocardial infarction (heart attack). Check out these models in augmented reality (AR) on your mobile devices here!

  • Predictive Model of
    Fastball Velocity

    I built a predictive model of fastball velocity based on ball spin rate and a variety of parameters gathered from biomechanical marker analyses. On average, I was able to predict the velocity of a fastball within +/- 2 mph. Check out the summary of my work here!
